Nodes

Blockchains are (unsurprisingly) made of blocks.

Each block is identified by its unique "hash" - a fingerprint created by running its data through an irreversible algorithm.

Aside from the hash, each block may also contain:

  • A timestamp — this tells us when the block was created.
  • The Merkle root — a hash of all previous transactions on the blockchain, right from the original ("genesis") node.
  • Data — this is where we store information, such as transactions or smart contracts (more on these later).
  • The hash of the previous block in the chain.

Pay attention to that last point — because each block contains the hash of the previous block, they're linked together in sequence, creating a "chain" of blocks.

But it's the growth of this chain where things get interesting.

Which leads us to forks.
