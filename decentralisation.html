Decentralisation

Humans can't survive without a brain.

Without the right neural inputs, vital processes fail, and take the rest of the body with them.

But what if we were designed differently?

What if, instead of a single brain, we each had millions (or even billions) of tiny brains, dispersed all throughout the world?

One thing's for sure: we'd be much harder to kill.

This is the idea behind decentralisation. Our million-brain analogy is imperfect, but it makes a lot more sense when you replace "brains" with "computers".

A decentralised system has no single source of control. Instead, decisions are distributed across all nodes in the network.

Blockchains are one such system.

They're public, immutable, decentralised ledgers of information, kept alive by the cumulative inputs of each participant.

The idea is to create a central source of truth that isn't owned by any single person. A global, unfakeable ground truth.
